## STEP 4 — CRITICAL THINKING LAYER

### Gaps, Assumptions, or Weaknesses

**1. Overgeneralization: "The Iranian regime has no popular support"**
The secular Persian-Canadian diaspora is a self-selected, politically active group — they fled or emigrated precisely because of ideological opposition. Inferring Iranian domestic opinion from diaspora sentiment is methodologically flawed. The 2022 Mahsa Amini protests showed real dissent, but regime collapse did not follow. Internal Iranian polling (where available) shows complex, stratified views.

**2. Missing Data: Implementation timeline for resource deregulation**
Poilievre presents resource unlocking as a near-term policy lever. No timeline, cost estimate, or infrastructure gap analysis is provided. Major Canadian resource projects (Trans Mountain pipeline, LNG Canada) have taken 10–15 years from conception to operation. The gap between "remove bureaucracy" and "deliver oil to US refineries" is operationally enormous.

**3. Logical Leap: Deterrence non-functionality in Iran**
The claim that Iran's theocratic ideology makes deterrence non-functional is a significant logical leap. Historical evidence shows the Iranian government has repeatedly chosen pragmatic survival over theological imperatives (e.g., accepting the 1988 ceasefire with Iraq, the 2015 JCPOA, multiple de-escalation moments with the US). The regime may use apocalyptic rhetoric instrumentally without genuinely acting on it.

**4. Survivorship Bias: "Canada has always been a reliable partner"**
Poilievre presents Canadian-
