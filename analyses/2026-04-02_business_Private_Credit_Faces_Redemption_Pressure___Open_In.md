### 3–5 Primary Arguments

**1. Private Credit's Structural Liquidity Mismatch Is Now Visible**
Blue Owl's tech-focused BDC received redemption requests of 41% of AUM — the largest recorded figure in this vehicle class. KKR exceeded its redemption limit and is paying out. The core issue is not credit performance per se, but a mismatch between semi-liquid product structures (5% quarterly redemption caps) and investor expectations of on-demand liquidity. This was a foreseeable design failure.

**2. Software Exposure Is the Hidden Credit Risk in Private BDCs**
Oaktree's Co-CEO Armen Panossian explicitly flagged that pre-2022 vintage software loans — originated before ChatGPT — carry AI displacement risk that has not been fully marked down. With some BDC portfolios 30–40% in software, a 5–10% binary loss scenario on AI-disrupted companies could generate 15%+ equity hits on leveraged vehicles. This is a slow-moving but potentially severe credit event.

**3. The Strait of Hormuz Closure Is a Global Supply Chain Event, Not Just an Oil Story**
J.P. Morgan estimates a four-month minimum to restore energy flows to 99% of prior levels, even after a ceasefire. Refined product disruptions are already causing recessionary-type impacts in Asia. The lag effect means oil price elevation persists well beyond any diplomatic resolution, with pass-through to U.S. consumer spending (estimated 0.5 percentage points of disposable income impact from gasoline alone).

**4. Markets Are Surprisingly Resilient — But for a Specific, Concentrated Reason**
The S&P 500 is down only ~4–5% since the Iran conflict began, despite oil doubling and the Strait closure. Goldman Sachs' Ben Snyder attributes this to AI capex tailwinds: tech and AI infrastructure companies represent ~40% of S&P revenues, dwarfing energy-exposed sectors (~20%). As long as hyperscaler capex continues, the index has a structural shock absorber.

**5. The "Retail Democratization" of Alternatives Is Now Its First Real Stress Test**
The expansion of private credit into retail-accessible interval funds and semi-liquid BDCs — the "next frontier of growth" for Blackstone, Apollo, Blue Owl, KKR — is facing its first meaningful redemption cycle. The industry's response (enforcing 5% caps, reassuring messaging) mirrors the 2022 Blackstone BREIT episode, but the current environment adds AI credit risk on top of geopolitical macro pressure.
