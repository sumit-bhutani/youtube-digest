### Incentive Misalignment

- No acknowledgment that: (a) the substance is unscheduled in some jurisdictions but Schedule I in others; (b) adverse events are documented; (c) the "surrender" skill may not be accessible to everyone, particularly those with trauma, anxiety disorders, or certain psychiatric diagnoses.

# Where This Video Is Directionally Wrong (If It Is)

### 1. The Potency Comparison Is Misleading
**What was said:** "5–10x more powerful than DMT."
**What is directionally wrong:** Psychedelic "potency" is not a single-axis measure. DMT and 5-MeO-DMT work through overlapping but distinct receptor profiles (5-HT2A agonism vs. broader serotonergic and sigma receptor activity), have different durations, different subjective character, and different adverse event profiles. Reducing this to a multiplier is pharmacologically inaccurate and potentially dangerous if it encourages dose extrapolation.

### 2. The Surrender Binary Oversimplifies a Complex Phenomenological Space
**What was said:** You either panic and resist, or you surrender and reach bliss.
**What is directionally wrong:** Published phenomenological research (e.g., Belser et al., 2017; Timmermann et al., 2021) identifies a much richer and more variable experiential landscape — including mixed states, incomplete dissolution, prolonged neutral or ambiguous experiences, and adverse outcomes that persist regardless of surrender disposition. The binary is a narrative convenience, not a phenomenological truth.

### 3. The "Raw Consciousness/Intelligence" Frame Conflates Phenomenology with Ontology
**What was said:** "You experience raw consciousness and raw intelligence."
**What is directionally wrong:** This is a philosophical interpretation of a subjective state, not a description of what is actually encountered. The same state has been interpreted as "contact with entities," "access to the unconscious," "neurological noise," and "dissolution of the default mode network" by different researchers and users. Johnson presents one interpretive frame as if it were the thing itself. This matters because it shapes how audiences conceptualize the experience and what products/practices they pursue as a result.

### 4. The Implicit "Anyone Can Do This" Frame Is Dangerous
**What was said (implicitly):** By presenting the experience as navigable through personal disposition alone, the clip implies broad accessibility.
**What is directionally wrong:** 5-MeO-DMT is contraindicated for individuals with personal or family history of psychosis, certain cardiac conditions, and those on serotonergic medications (serotonin syndrome risk). The absence of any such caveat in a clip reaching millions of viewers is a genuine public health concern, not merely a narrative oversight.
